*B.1 Task 1Study ParticipantUsing a dedicated, validated and commercially available electronic stethoscope (3MTM Littman CORE Digital Stethoscope), the study participant is examined by the attending physician. 
*B.1 Task 2Acquire Sound ClipsCough sound clips will be acquired for each subject per the protocol validated in other work by Dr Rameau. Subjects will be in seated position and asked to perform a maximal voluntary cough following the example of the trained research associate. The subject is instructed to take a deep, full breath in then cough as forcefully as possible. The maneuver is repeated to complete 3 voluntary coughs that are reproducible and of sufficient sound quality.
*B.1 Task 3DocumentationThe physician performing the examination documents breathing pattern, description of inspiratory and expiratory breath sounds, presence or absence of adventitious breath sounds and quantification of intensity of adventitious breath sounds when present (see list below). For the sleep apnea patients, a recording will be performed during in lab and home testing with polysomnogram split-night study. Recordings will include both the diagnostic portion of the night as well as the titration. A high-fidelity recorder will be placed on the head of the bed for in lab studies and in the case of home study on the chest strap.
